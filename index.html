Life in the Pride

Lions are unique among big cats because they live in social groups called prides. A pride consists of related lionesses, their cubs, and a few dominant males. The lionesses are the primary hunters, while the males protect the territory.


Cubs are raised by the entire pride, learning to hunt and survive in the wild. As they grow, young males eventually leave to form their own groups, continuing the cycle of life.
